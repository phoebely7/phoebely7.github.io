Research Assistant

Hello! My name is Phoebe Ly and I recently graduated from UC Riverside with a B.S in Pure Statistics with a minor in Computer Science. I am currently a Research Assistant in Professor David Lobell's Lab in the Center of Food Security and the Environment (FSE) at Stanford University.

My research interests include examining soil mechanics and agriculture, using modern tools such as remote sensing and machine learning, to understand its impact on public health. This past summer, I had the privilege of being a Stanford SURGE scholar where I got to explore this field.

During my time in college, I wanted to make efforts towards bridging the gap between the undergraduate students and the faculty within the Department of Statistics. Toward this aim, I led a number of research projects that gave students the opportunity to connect with professors, as well as host Department panels to foster conversations. Addionally, I served as a member of the Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion committee to brainstorm initiatives to further improve the Department.

During my free time, I love to create digital art, bake croissants, and purchase overpriced drinks at my local coffee shop!
